    Gene set SNV & survival module provides summary of the result of survival difference between gene set mutant and wide type.

  • Methods

    • The SNV data and clinical survival data were collected from TCGA database. SNV data and clinical survival data were merged by sample barcode.

    The gene set SNV represents the integrated SNV status of inputted gene set for each sample. A sample is classified into Mutant group only when at least one gene in the input gene set is mutated in the sample. If all genes in inputted gene set have no SNV in a sample, this sample is classified into WT group. Only samples have deleterious mutants are included in Mutant group.

    Survival analysis only performed for groups with >2 samples. And at least 2 groups are needed.

    R package survival was used to fit survival time and survival status two groups. Cox Proportional-Hazards model and Logrank tests were performed to test the survival difference between groups.

    Note that the users may could not get the survival results in sevaral cases, , and there are some suggestions for survival analysis:

    • 1. The OS data are avaliable for all cancer types, while survival analysis for OS is not recommded for DLBC, PCPG, TGCT, and THYM (Liu et al, Cell, 2018).
    • 2. The PFS data are not avaliable for LAML. Survival analysis for PFS is not recommded for PCPG (Liu et al, Cell, 2018).
    • 3. The DSS data are not avaliable for LAML. Survival analysis for DSS is not recommded for DLBC, PCPG, PRAD, TGCT, THCA, and THYM(Liu et al, Cell, 2018).
    • 4. The DFI data are not avaliable for LAML, SKCM, THYM, and UVM. Survival analysis for DFI is not recommded for DLBC, GBM, KICH, MESO, PCPG, and READ(Liu et al, Cell, 2018).
    • 5. The inputted gene set do not have enough mutation, which lead the number of groups (have > 2 samples) that used for survival comparing is not enough (two groups are needed).
    • 6. HR equal to 1.
  • Interpretation

    • GSCA provides two forms of results, including table and figure.

    • The result table:

      • Presents detailed information about Cancer type, Survival type, Hazard Ratio, Cox P value, Logrank P value, Higher risk of death.

      Survival type including OS, PFS, DSS, and DFI.

      Hazard Ratio: the risk of death of Mutant group compared with WT group.

      Higher risk of death indicates the group with higher risk of death. When the Hazard ratio of Mutant group compared with low WT group is greather than 1, we think Mutant group has higher risk of death, else WT group has higher risk of death.

    • GSCA provides result figures including:
      • Kaplan-Meier curves:

        • By clicking buttons in table, GSCA draws Kaplan-Meier curves between WT and Mutant (deleterious mutation) groups of specific gene in specific cancer.

      • Bubble plot:

        • Presents the Hazard ratio and Cox p value through bubble color and size. The column is selected cancer types and the row is survival type (OS, PFS, DSS, and DFI). The bubble color from blue to red represents the hazard ratio from low to high, bubble size is positively correlate with the Cox P value significance.
