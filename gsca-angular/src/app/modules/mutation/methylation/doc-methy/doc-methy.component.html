    Differential methylation module provides differential analysis of methylation between tumor and normal sample groups.

  • Methods

    • Illumina HumanMethylation 450k level 3 data were downloaded from TCGA database. In total, 14 cancer types (THCA, KIRP, BLCA, LIHC, HNSC, BRCA, LUAD, PRAD, ESCA, KICH, LUSC, KIRC, STAD, COAD), which have more than 10 paired tumor and adjacent non-tumor samples, were selected and processed into this analysis.

    Generally, there are multiple methylation sites in the region of one gene. Therefore, there are multiple tags store the methylation level of each site. Before differential methylation analysis, we performed correlation analysis to filter the sites most negatively correlate with gene expression into this analysis.

    The p-value was estimated by t-test and was further adjusted by FDR.

  • Interpretation

    • GSCA provides two forms of results, including the tables and figures.

    • Table:

      • The table presents detailed information about Cancer type, Gene symbol, Tag, Methylation(Tumor-Normal), Trend, P value and FDR.

      Tag: the tag id of methylation site within the region of the specific gene.

      Methylation(Tumor-Normal): the mean methylation of tumor samples subtracts that of normal samples.

      Trend: changes of methylation in tumor samples compared with normal samples.

    • GSCA provides result figures, including:
      • Bubble plot:

        • The bubble plot presents the fold change and FDR through bubble color and size. The row is the gene symbol and the column is the selected cancer types. The bubble color from blue to red represents the methylation difference between tumor and normal samples. The size dot is positively correlate with the FDR significance.       • Box plot:
