    This module summarizes the profile of inputted genes' SNV in selected cancer types.

  • Methods

    • The SNV data of 10234 samples from 33 cancer types were collected from the TCGA database.

    Seven types of mutation were included in this analysis: Missense_Mutation, Nonsense_Mutation, Frame_Shift_Ins, Splice_Site, Frame_Shift_Del, In_Frame_Del, In_Frame_Ins. We call these types of mutations deleterious mutations. And the non-deleterious mutations include Silent, Intron, IGR, 3'UTR, 5'UTR, 3'Flank, and 5'Flank.

  • Interpretation

    • GSCA provides two forms of results, including tables and figures.

    • Table:

      • The table presents detailed information about Cancer type, Gene symbol, deleterious mutation, Non-deleterious mutation, Total sample size, and Percentage.

      Deleterious mutation: the number of samples with at least one deleterious mutation site on the region of a specific gene.

      Non-deleterious mutation: the number of samples with a non-deleterious mutation site on the region of a specific gene.

      Total sample size: the number of samples with SNV mutation data.

      Percentage: the percentage of samples with deleterious mutations.

    • GSCA provides result figures, including:
      • Lollipop plot:

        • By clicking the gene symbol button, GSCA will give you a lollipop plot that presents the mutation site, type, and count of a specific gene in the sample set of a specific cancer type.

      • Percentage heatmap:

        • Heatmap summarizes the frequency of deleterious mutations in selected cancer types.

      • Summary plot:

        • This plot summarizes the count and type of variants in the sample and gene levels.

        Plot 1 (Variant Classification): the count of each type of deleterious mutation (Missense_Mutation, Nonsense_Mutation, Frame_Shift_Ins, Splice_Site, Frame_Shift_Del, In_Frame_Del, In_Frame_Ins) of inputted gene set in selected cancer types.

        Plot 2 (Variant Type): the SNP and DEL count of inputted gene set in selected cancer types.

        Plot 3 (SNV class): the count of each SNV class of inputted gene set ins selected cancer types.

        Plot 4 (Variants per sample): the count of variants in each sample. One bar indicates a sample, and the color of the bar corresponds to the color of variant classification.

        Plot 5 (Variant Classification summary): box plot shows the distribution of the count of each variant classification in the sample set of selected cancer types. The color of the box corresponds to the color of variant classification.

        Plot 6 (Top 10 mutated genes): the count and percentage of variants in the top 10 mutated genes.

      • Oncoplot:

        • This plot (also called waterfall plot) presents the mutation distribution of the top 10 (when num of gene set <10, show all) mutated genes from inputted gene set in the sample set of selected cancers. It also provides the classification of SNV types.

        In this plot, only samples that have at least one SNV are included. The following plot shows that the top 10 genes are altered in 213 samples, and 251 samples have at least one SNV in the input gene set.

      • Titv plot:

        • Classifies the mutations into Transitions(Ti) and transversions(Tv). Transitions are interchanges of two-ring purines (A<->G) or of one-ring pyrimidines (C<->T): therefore, they involve bases of similar shapes. Transversions are interchanges of a purine for pyrimidine bases, which involve the exchange of one-ring and two-ring structures. Although there are twice as many possible transversions, transition mutations are generated at a higher frequency than transversions in the whole gene because of the molecular mechanisms by which they are generated. As well, transitions are less likely to result in amino acid substitutions (due to "wobble") and are therefore more likely to persist as "silent substitutions" in populations as single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs).

        This plot shows the distribution of Ti and Tv of inputted gene set in the sample set of selected cancer types.

        Plot 1(upper left): the box plot shows the distribution of the SVN class of inputted gene set in the sample set of selected cancer types. In most cases, one type of SNV class could not exist in more than half of the samples. Therefore, the median value (thick black line) of the box plot generally locate at 0, especially when the size of an inputted gene set is small or when the frequency of SNV of the gene set is low.

        Plot 2(upper right): the box plot shows the distribution of Ti (A<->G and C<->T) and Tv (others) of inputted gene set in the sample set of selected cancer types.

        Plot 3(below) shows the frequency of SNV class of inputted gene set for each sample.
