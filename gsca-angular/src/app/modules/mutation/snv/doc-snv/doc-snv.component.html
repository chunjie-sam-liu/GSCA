  • Interpretation

    • GSCA provides two forms of results, including table and figure.

    The SNV data were collected from TCGA.

    • The result table:

      • Presents detailed information about Cancer type, Gene symbol, Effective mutation, Non-effective mutation, Total sample size and Percentage.

      Effective mutation: the number of samples have at least one effective mutation site. The effective mutations including Missense_Mutation, Nonsense_Mutation, Frame_Shift_Ins, Splice_Site, Frame_Shift_Del, In_Frame_Del and In_Frame_Ins.

      Effective mutation: the number of samples have at least one effective mutation site. The effective mutations including Missense_Mutation, Nonsense_Mutation, Frame_Shift_Ins, Splice_Site, Frame_Shift_Del, In_Frame_Del and In_Frame_Ins.

      Non-effective mutation: the number of samples only have non-effective mutation site. The non-effective mutations including Silent, Intron, IGR, 3'UTR, 5'UTR, 3'Flank, and 5'Flank.

      Total sample size: the number of samples with SNV mutation data.

      Percentage: the percentage of samples with effective mutations.

    • GSCA provides result figures including:
      • Lollipop plot:

        • By clicking the gene symbol button, GSCA will give you a lollipop plot presents the mutation site, type and count of a specific gene.

      • Percentage heatmap:

        • Summarize the frequency of effective mutations in selected caner types.

      • Summary plot:

        • Summarizes the count and type of variants in sample and gene levels.

      • Oncoplot:

        • Also called waterfall plot, presents the mutation distribution of top 10 (when num of gene set <10, show all) mutated genes in your gene set. Also provides the classification of SNV types.

      • Titv plot:

        • Classifies the mutations into Transitions(Ti) and transversions(Tv). Transitions are interchanges of two-ring purines (A<->G) or of one-ring pyrimidines (C<->T): they therefore involve bases of similar shape. Transversions are interchanges of purine for pyrimidine bases, which therefore involve exchange of one-ring and two-ring structures. Although there are twice as many possible transversions, because of the molecular mechanisms by which they are generated, transition mutations are generated at higher frequency than transversions. As well, transitions are less likely to result in amino acid substitutions (due to "wobble"), and are therefore more likely to persist as "silent substitutions" in populations as single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs).
