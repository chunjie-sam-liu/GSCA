Introduction

GSCA (Gene Set Cancer Analysis) is an updated version of GSCALite, which is a web-based analysis platform for gene set cancer analysis relate to mRNA expression, mutation, immune infiltrates and drug resistance. The platform mainly based on TCGA and GDSC database.
The gene set alteration on DNA or RNA are received more and more attentions in recent years, which may be important in cancer initiation, progress, diagnosis, prognosis and therapy. As the cancer genomics big data available, it is very useful and urgent to provide a platform for gene set analysis in cancer.
By using GSCA, experimental biologists can easily explore the large TCGA and drug response datasets, test hypotheses relate to genomics changes, and their association with clinical outcome and immune infiltration in a higher resolution.

Abstract Summary

 Updates of GSCA compared with GSCALite

GSCA retained most of the functions in the GSCALite. Besides, in this version, more datasets and functions were added:

  1. Gene set estimation

    2. This function allows users to estimate the overall estimation of the gene set in the aspect of mRNA expression, SNV and CNV levels. This make it possible to carry out calculation in the gene set level instead of one gene. For instance, GSVA analysis was performed to estimate the overall expression level of gene set for each TCGA sample.

  2. Association with immune infiltrates

    3. This function allows users to estimate the association of genomic features (RNA sequencing expression, SNV, CNV and methylation) with 25 immune cells infiltration, evaluated by ImmuCellAI.

  3. Detailed single gene result

    4. This function provides results of single gene for batch calculation, which avoid fussy input options when the users have plenty of candidate genes.

  4. Cancer TNM Stage

    5. This function allows the comparison of gene expression between TNM stages. A trend analysis was utilized to get the trend of expression as the disease progresses.

Quick start

  1. Click "Example" button to get example inputs. Or enter the official symbols of gene set with more than 2 genes and less than 50 in the "Input your interested gene set".

  2. Select at least one cancer type in the "Select cancer types".

  3. Select at least one analysis.

  4. Click "Submit" button to get results of interest gene set.

 Try out example and Results interpretation

By click "Example" button, GSCA will search gene set "A2M, ACE, ANGPT2, BPI, CD1B, CDR1, EGR2, EGR3, HBEGF, HERPUD1, MCM2, PCTP, PODXL, PPY, PTGS2, RCAN1, SLC4A7, THBD" in cancer types "KICH, KIRC, KIRP, LUAD, LUSC". We will give some example results and their interpretation.

Expression module

The expression module contains both single gene level and gene set level analysis. The gene set expression level is present by GSVA score.

  • Single gene level
    • Differential expression

      • This part analyze the differential expression between tumor and adjacent normal samples. The bubble color from purple to red represent the fold change between tumor vs normal. The size dot indicates the significance. The dot was filtered by the fold change (FC>2) and significance (FDR < 0.05). Users can found that A2M is significantly downregulate in LUSC.

      Through clicking the buttons "A2M" or "LUSC" in the specific row of the result table, users can get the detailed expression distribution of A2M in LUSC or pan-cancer.

    • GSEA enrichment

      • Bar plot and enrichment plot give the enrichment score of gene set against all genes. This result gives the users the distribution of gene set expression changes among all genes(backgroud). By clicking "LUSC" button in the result table, the users can find that among the inputed gene set, most of them are downregulated in LUSC, distributed on the right blue side of enrichment plot.

    • Stage and expression

      • This part estimate the expression difference between TNM stages and the expression tendency from stage I to IV. The bubble color from blue to red and the size represent the FDR significance. Black outline border indicates FDR < 0.05. Users can found that PODXL is significantly different among stage in KIRC.

      Through clicking the buttons "PODXL" in the specific row of the result table, users can get the detailed expression distribution of PODXL in KIRC stages by box plot.

      Trend of gene expression from stage I to stage IV is present as heat mapand trend plot.

    • Subtype and expression

      • This part estimate the expression difference between subtypes. The color from white to red represent the FDR significance in heat map. Users can found that CD1B is significantly different among subtypes in LUAD. Through clicking the buttons "CD1B" in the specific row of the result table, users can get the detailed expression distribution of CD1B in LUAD subtypes by box plot.

    • Survival and expression

      • This part estimate the OS and PFS difference between high and low gene expression. The color from blue to red represent the hazard ratio, size represents statistical significance in bubble plot. Black outline border indicates Logrank P < 0.05. Users can found that SLC4A7 is significantly associate with KICH patientsâ€™ OS. Through clicking the buttons "SLC4A7" in the specific row of the result table, users can get the Kaplan-Meier survival curve between high and low SLC4A7 expression in KICH.

  • Gene set level
    • Differential GSVA score

      • The boxplotcompare the GSVA score of inputed gene set between tumor and normal samples. The users can fing that the overall expression level of example gene set are significantly downregulated in tumor.

    • Stage and GSVA score

      • The boxplotcompare the GSVA score of inputed gene set among TNM stages. The trend plot persents the trend of middle GSVA score from stage I to stage IV. Users can find that the overall expression level of example gene set are trend to upregulate in KICH, KIRP and LUSC, and trend to downregulate in KIRC and LUAD, but without statistical significance (Tend P > 0.05, estimated by Mann-Kendall Trend Test).

    • Subtype and GSVA score

      • The boxplotcompare the GSVA score of inputed gene set among cancer subtypes. The users can find that the GSVA score significantly upregulated in LUAD subtypes, from subtype 1 to 6.

    • Survival and GSVA score

      • The bubble plot shows the summary of HR and logrank p of GSVA score among cancers. Black outline border indicates Logrank P < 0.05. There are no significant difference between high and low GSVA score groups in each cancer survival. By clicking button "KIRP" in result table, users can get the Kaplan-Meier survival curve.

Mutation module

In this module, users can easily explore the statistic information of mutation and their association with survival and mRNA expression.

  • SNV (Single Nucleotide Mutation)

    • SNV module presents the SNV frequency and variant types of the gene set in selected cancer types. The effects of single gene and gene set SNV to survival.

    • SNV summary

      • Give the SNV summary of each gene in specific cancers in mutiple forms, including table, heatmap, summary plot, oncoplotand Titv plot.

      • By clicking gene symbol button in the table result, a Lollipop plot gives a gene mutation site, type and count.
      • The heatmap summarize the SNV frequency in cancer
      • The summary plot gives the count and type of variants in sample, gene levels.
      • The oncoplot, also called waterfall plot presents the waterfall plots, gives a mutation distribution of top 10 (when num of gene set <10, show all) mutated genes in your gene set and a SNV classification of SNV types
      • The Titv plot classifes the mutations into Transitions(Ti) and transversions(Tv). DNA substitution mutations are of two types.

        • Transitions are interchanges of two-ring purines (A<->G) or of one-ring pyrimidines (C<->T): they therefore involve bases of similar shape. Transversions are interchanges of purine for pyrimidine bases, which therefore involve exchange of one-ring and two-ring structures. Although there are twice as many possible transversions, because of the molecular mechanisms by which they are generated, transition mutations are generated at higher frequency than transversions. As well, transitions are less likely to result in amino acid substitutions (due to "wobble"), and are therefore more likely to persist as "silent substitutions" in populations as single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs).

    • Single gene SNV and survival

      • This function provide summary and single gene survival result between mutant and wide type. The color from blue to red represent the hazard ratio, size represents statistical significance in bubble plot. Black outline border indicates Logrank P < 0.05.

      By clicking the button "A2M" in the specific row of the result table, users can get the Kaplan-Meier survival curve between A2M mutant and wide type in KIRC.

    • Gene set SNV and survival

      • This function provide summary and single cancer result between gene set mutant and wide type. The color from blue to red represent the hazard ratio, size represents statistical significance in bubble plot. Black outline border indicates Logrank P < 0.05.

      By clicking button "KIRC" in the result table, user can get the Users can get the Kaplan-Meier survival curve between gene set mutant and wide type in KIRC.

  • CNV (Copy Number Variation)

    • On CNV module, the statistics of hetero-zygous and homozygous CNV of each cancer type are displayed, and correlation between gene expression and CNV of each gene in each cancer to help to analyze the gene expression significantly affected by CNV. In addition, the association between CNV aplification, deletion with survival.

    • CNV summary

      • Give the CNV summary of each gene in specific cancers in mutiple forms, including table, pie plot, bubble plot and oncoplot.

      • By clicking gene symbol button in the table result, a onco gives the distribution of CNV type and ratiosite for a gene.
      • CNV pie plot gives you a global profile that shows the constitute of Heterozygous/Homozygous CNV of each gene in each cancer. A pie represents the proportion of different types of CNV of one gene in one cancer, and different color represent different types of CNV.
      • Heterozygous CNV profile show users the percentage of heterozygous CNV, including amplification and deletion percentage of heterozygous CNV about each gene in each cancer. The heterozygous CNV is generally happened so the plot will full of points, but most of researchs such as cBioPortal mainly focus on homozygous CNV in the present.
      • Homozygous CNV profile show users the percentage of homozygous CNV, including amplification and deletion percentage of homozygous CNV about each gene in each cancer. Most of researchs such as cBioPortal mainly focus on homozygous CNV in the present.
    • CNV and expression

      • From this, users can get genes whose expression significantly correlate with CNV. Blue bubbles represent a negative correlation, and red bubbles represent positive correlation, he deeper of color, the higher the correlation. The size of the point represents statistic significance, the bigger of size, the more statistic significant.

      By clicking the button "A2M" in the specific row of the result table, users can get the Kaplan-Meier survival curve between A2M CNV (homo amplification and homo deletion) and wide type.
