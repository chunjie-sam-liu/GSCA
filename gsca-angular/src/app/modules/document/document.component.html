Introduction

GSCA (Gene Set Cancer Analysis) is an updated version of GSCALite, which is a web-based analysis platform for gene set cancer analysis relate to mRNA expression, mutation, immune infiltrates and drug resistance. The platform mainly based on TCGA and GDSC database.
The gene set alteration on DNA or RNA are received more and more attentions in recent years, which may be important in cancer initiation, progress, diagnosis, prognosis and therapy. As the cancer genomics big data available, it is very useful and urgent to provide a platform for gene set analysis in cancer.
By using GSCA, experimental biologists can easily explore the large TCGA and drug response datasets, test hypotheses relate to genomics changes, and their association with clinical outcome and immune infiltration in a higher resolution.

Abstract Summary

 Quick start

  1. Click "Example" button to get example inputs. Or enter the official symbols of gene set with more than 2 genes and less than 50 in the “Input your interested gene set”.

  2. Select at least one cancer type in the "Select cancer types".

  3. Select at least one analysis.

  4. Click "Submit" button to get results of interest gene set.

 Updates of GSCA compared with GSCALite

GSCA retained most of the functions in the GSCALite. Besides, in this version, more datasets and functions were added:

  1. Gene set estimation

    This function allows users to estimate the overall estimation of the gene set in the aspect of mRNA expression, SNV and CNV levels. This make it possible to carry out calculation in the gene set level instead of one gene. For instance, GSVA analysis was performed to estimate the overall expression level of gene set for each TCGA sample.

  2. Association with immune infiltrates

    This function allows users to estimate the association of genomic features (RNA sequencing expression, SNV, CNV and methylation) with 25 immune cells infiltration, evaluated by ImmuCellAI.

  3. Detailed single gene result

    This function provides results of single gene for batch calculation, which avoid fussy input options when the users have plenty of candidate genes.

  4. Cancer TNM Stage

    This function allows the comparison of gene expression between TNM stages. A trend analysis was utilized to get the trend of expression as the disease progresses.

      • xx cancer types

      • xx cancer types

      • including molecular subtypes or clustered subtypes (numbered)

    SNV data

    • Statistic
      • 33 cancer types
      • 8,663 samples
    • Data process
      • Mutant including 7 types of mutations: Missense_Mutation, Nonsense_Mutation, Frame_Shift_Ins, Splice_Site, Frame_Shift_Del, In_Frame_Del, In_Frame_Ins.
      • Wide type(WT): others.

    CNV data

    • Statistic
      • 33 cancer types
      • 11,495 samples
    • Data process
      • GISTICS2.0
      • Hete Amp: heterozygous amplification, CNV = 1
      • Hete Del: heterozygous deletion, CNV = -1
      • Homo Amp: homozygous amplification, CNV = 2
      • Homo Del: homozygous deletion, CNV = -2

    Methylation data

    • Statistic
      • 33 cancer types
      • 10,129 samples
    • Data process
      • Calculation based on methylation beta value
      • 14 cancers have over ten paired tumor and normal samples

  • Drug data

    Therapeutics Response Portal (CTRP)

    • 481 small molecules IC50
    • 1001 cell lines
    • 18900 genes

    Genomics of Drug Sensitivity in Cancer (GDSC)

    • 265 small molecules IC50
    • 860 cell lines
    • 17419 genes

  • Immune infiltrates

    • Statistic
      • TCGA 10,995 samples
      • 33 cancer types
      • 25 immune cells
    • Data process
      • Based on mRNA expression data, ImmuCellAI was performed to get immune cells infiltrates
      • 25 immune cells
 Try out example and Results interpretation

By click "Example" button, GSCA will search gene set "A2M, ACE, ANGPT2, BPI, CD1B, CDR1, EGR2, EGR3, HBEGF, HERPUD1, MCM2, PCTP, PODXL, PPY, PTGS2, RCAN1, SLC4A7, THBD" in cancer types "KICH, KIRC, KIRP, LUAD, LUSC". We will give some example results and their interpretation.

Expression module

The expression module contains both single gene level and gene set level analysis. The gene set expression level is present by GSVA score.

  • Single gene level
    • Differential expression

      • This part analyze the differential expression between tumor and adjacent normal samples. The bubble color from purple to red represent the fold change between tumor vs normal. The size dot indicates the significance. The dot was filtered by the fold change (FC>2) and significance (FDR < 0.05). Users can found that A2M is significantly downregulate in LUSC.

      Through clicking the buttons “A2M” or “LUSC” in the specific row of the result table, users can get the detailed expression distribution of A2M in LUSC or pan-cancer.

    • GSEA enrichment

      • Bar plot and enrichment plot give the enrichment score of gene set against all genes. This result gives the users the distribution of gene set expression changes among all genes(backgroud). By clicking “LUSC” button in the result table, the users can find that among the inputed gene set, most of them are downregulated in LUSC, distributed on the right blue side of enrichment plot.

    • Stage and expression

      • This part estimate the expression difference between TNM stages and the expression tendency from stage I to IV. The bubble color from blue to red and the size represent the FDR significance. Black outline border indicates FDR < 0.05. Users can found that PODXL is significantly different among stage in KIRC.

      Through clicking the buttons “PODXL” in the specific row of the result table, users can get the detailed expression distribution of PODXL in KIRC stages by box plot.

      Trend of gene expression from stage I to stage IV is present as heat map and trend plot.

    • Subtype and expression

      • This part estimate the expression difference between subtypes. The color from white to red represent the FDR significance in heat map. Users can found that CD1B is significantly different among subtypes in LUAD. Through clicking the buttons “CD1B” in the specific row of the result table, users can get the detailed expression distribution of CD1B in LUAD subtypes by box plot.

    • Survival and expression

      • This part estimate the OS and PFS difference between high and low gene expression. The color from blue to red represent the hazard ratio, size represents statistical significance in bubble plot. Black outline border indicates Logrank P < 0.05. Users can found that SLC4A7 is significantly associate with KICH patients’ OS. Through clicking the buttons “SLC4A7” in the specific row of the result table, users can get the Kaplan-Meier survival curve between high and low SLC4A7 expression in KICH.

  • Gene set level
    • Differential GSVA score

      • The boxplot compare the GSVA score of inputed gene set between tumor and normal samples. The users can fing that the overall expression level of example gene set are significantly downregulated in tumor.

    • Stage and GSVA score

      • The boxplot compare the GSVA score of inputed gene set among TNM stages. The trend plot persents the trend of middle GSVA score from stage I to stage IV. Users can find that the overall expression level of example gene set are trend to upregulate in KICH, KIRP and LUSC, and trend to downregulate in KIRC and LUAD, but without statistical significance (Tend P > 0.05, estimated by Mann-Kendall Trend Test).

    • Subtype and GSVA score

      • The boxplot compare the GSVA score of inputed gene set among cancer subtypes. The users can find that the GSVA score significantly upregulated in LUAD subtypes, from subtype 1 to 6.

    • Survival and GSVA score

      • The bubble plot shows the summary of HR and logrank p of GSVA score among cancers. Black outline border indicates Logrank P < 0.05. There are no significant difference between high and low GSVA score groups in each cancer survival. By clicking button “KIRP” in result table, users can get the Kaplan-Meier survival curve.

Immune module

In Immune module, users can easily explore the correlation between immune cells infiltrates and genomics features, including:

  • mRNA expression
  • SNV
  • CNV
  • Methylation

In these modules, bubble plot presents the summary of the correlation for each cancer. The bubble color from blue to red represent the correlation. Bubble size are correlate with FDR significancec. Black outline border indicates FDR < 0.05. By clicking gene symbol buttons in tables, users can get the detailed result of single gene through scatter plot with fitting line (mRNA expression, CNV and methylation), or boxplot (SNV).
