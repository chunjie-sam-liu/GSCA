Introduction

Gene Set Cancer Analysis (GSCA) is an enhanced version of GSCALite, which is a database to search, investigate and explore the gene set cancer analysis relate to mRNA expression, mutation, immune infiltration and drug resistance. The alterations of DNA, RNA and immune microenvironment could contribute to the cancer initiation, progress, diagnosis, prognosis, therapy. In bio-big data era, individual causal gene signal could be masked by massive background noises. A set of genes or compiled gene set scores from multiple dimensional data across a large number patients per stage could represent a snap shot of the underlying cancer process. In this enhanced GSCA, we provide a series of services to perform gene set genomic (Expression, SNV, CNV and methylation) and immunogenomic (24 immune cells) analyses. Besides, combining clinical information and small molecular drugs, user could mine candidate biomarkers and valuable small drugs for better experimental design and further clinical trials. GSCA integrates over 10,000 multi-dimensional genomic data across 33 cancer types from TCGA and over 750 small molecule drugs from GDSC and CTRP. Immunogenomic analysis was performed by our ImmuCellAI algorithm with 24 immunes cells.

In this enhanced GSCA, we uniquely provide gene set search and compile scores by gene set enrichment analysis (GSEA) and gene set variation analysis (GSVA) to investigate genomic variations and clinical outcomes. Thus, the unique functions of GSCA are: (i) exploring correlations between SNV/CNV/methylation and expression score compiled by GSEA and GSVA of a gene set; (ii) investigating correlations between immune cell abundance and genomic variations of a gene set or patient’s clinical outcomes in cancer; (iii) prioritizing drugs by drug sensitivity of gene expression. In conclusion, The GSCA database provides a one-stop solution for experimental biologists to validate hypotheses and design better experiments.

GSCA compared with GSCALite

GSCA retained most of the functions in the GSCALite. Besides, in this version, more datasets and functions were added:

  1. Gene set estimation

    2. This function allows users to estimate the integrated estimation of the gene set in the aspect of mRNA expression, SNV and CNV levels. This make it possible to carry out calculation in the gene set level instead of single gene. For instance, GSVA analysis was performed to estimate the integrated level of gene set expression for each TCGA sample.

  2. Association with immune infiltration

    3. This function allows users to estimate the association of genomic features (RNA sequencing expression, SNV, CNV and methylation) with 24 immune cells infiltration, evaluated by ImmuCellAI.

  3. Detailed single gene result

    4. This function provides detailed results of single gene of batch calculation, which avoid fussy input options when the users have plenty of candidate genes. For example, if you want to get the KM survival plot of several genes in several cancers, simply enter the gene list and select the cancer types, then click the specific button to get the survival plot of a gene in a cancer.

  4. Cancer Stage

    5. This function allows the comparison of gene expression between stages. A trend analysis was utilized to get the trend of expression as the disease progresses. Four types of stage (pathologic, clinical, masaoka (for THYM only), and igcccg stage (for TGCT only)) data of 9478 tumor samples from 27 cancer types are used in stage analyses.

Quick start

  1. Click "Example" button to get example inputs. Or enter the official symbols of gene set with more than 2 genes and less than 50 in the "Input your interested gene set with 2-500 genes".

  2. Select at least one cancer type in the "Select cancer types".

  3. Select at least one analysis.

  4. Click "Search" button to get the results of your interest gene set.

    • GSEA enrichment

      • GSCA gives the degree to which the inputted gene set is overrepresented at the top or bottom of all genes ranked by gene expression fold change between tumor and normal samples. Bar plot and enrichment plot present the enrichment score of gene set against all 20000+ genes (background) in selected cancers. Data collection and process

  • The TCGA mRNA expression, CNV and methylation data were obtained from UCSC Xena. The TCGA SNV data were obtained from the Synapse (syn7824274). The drug sensitivity data are collected from GDSC and CTRP database.

    Statistics of samples in cancer types

    Samples that have competing risk for death from cancer were filtered out (for DSS and DFI data).

    mRNA sequencing data

    • Data process
      • 14 cancers have over ten paired tumor and normal samples.
      • RSEM normalized mRNA expression data were downloaded from harmonized TCGA PanCan Atlas data in UCSC Xena UCSC Xena, which has been normalized for removing batch effects.

    Clinical information

    • Survival
      • Including overall survival(OS), progression free survival(PFS), disease specific survival(DSS), and disease free survival(DFI).
    • Stage
      • Stage data were further grouped into stage I, II, III, and IV. Undefined stages, such as stage x, were excluded.
      • Contain 9478 tumor samples from 27 cancer types
      • Including pathologic, clinical, masaoka (for THYM only), and igcccg (for TGCT only) stage.
    • Subtype
      • including molecular subtypes or clustered subtypes (numbered)

    SNV data

    • Data process
      • Processed TCGA SNV data were downloaded from the Synapse database (id: syn7824274).
      • Non-synonymous mutations, including Missense_Mutation, Nonsense_Mutation, Frame_Shift_Ins, Splice_Site, Frame_Shift_Del, In_Frame_Del, In_Frame_Ins, were considered deleterious mutations.
      • Wide type(WT): others.
      • Only deleterious mutations were treated as mutants in modules related to SNV data.

    CNV data

    • Data process
      • GISTICS2.0
      • Hete Amp: heterozygous amplification, CNV = 1
      • Hete Del: heterozygous deletion, CNV = -1
      • Homo Amp: homozygous amplification, CNV = 2
      • Homo Del: homozygous deletion, CNV = -2

    Methylation data

    • Data process
      • Calculation based on methylation beta value
      • 14 cancers have over ten paired tumor and normal samples
