  • Methods

    • The Immune infiltration & CNV module is used to estimate the association between gene CNV (Copy Number Variation) and immune cells’ infiltrates through Spearman correlation analysis. The infiltrates of 24 immune cells were evaluated through ImmuCellAI.

  • Interpretation

    • GSCA provides two forms of results, including tables and figures.

    • Table:

      • GSCA presents detailed information about Cancer type, Gene symbol, Cell type, Correlation, and FDR.

      Cell type: there are 24 types of immune cells, such as Dendritic cell, B cell, and et al.. More details are available in the document

      Correlation: the Spearman correlation coefficient between specific gene CNV and specific immune cell’s infiltrate.

      FDR: the false discovery rate.

    • GSCA provides result figures, including:
      • Bubble plot:

        • By clicking the cancer type button, GSCA will give you a bubble plot that presents the summary of the correlation between inputted genes’ CNV and 24 immune cell types' infiltrates in selected cancer. Bubble size correlates with FDR significance. The black outline border indicates FDR ≤ 0.05.

      • Scatter plot:

        • By clicking gene symbol buttons in the tables, users can get the correlation result between a single gene’s CNV and a specific immune cell's infiltrate through a scatter plot, with a fitting line.
