  • Methods

    • GSVA and pathway activity module presents the correlation between GSVA score and pathway activity, which is defined by pathway scores.

    Reverse phase protein array (RPPA) data from (TCPA database) were used to calculate the pathway activity score of 10 cancer related pathways for 7876 samples (from the TCGA database) from 32 cancer types. RPPA is a high-throughput antibody-based technique with procedures similar to that of Western blots. Proteins are extracted from tumor tissue or cultured cells, denatured by SDS, and printed on nitrocellulose-coated slides followed by an antibody probe.

    The pathways GSCA included are TSC/mTOR, RTK, RAS/MAPK, PI3K/AKT, Hormone ER, Hormone AR, EMT, DNA Damage Response, Cell Cycle, and Apoptosis pathways. They are all famous cancer-related pathways.

    RBN RPPA data were median-centered and normalized by the standard deviation across all samples for each component to obtain the relative protein level. The pathway activity score is then the sum of the relative protein level of all positive regulatory components minus that of negative regulatory components in a particular pathway (R. Akbani et al.).

    GSVA score represents the variation of gene set activity over a specific cancer sample population in an unsupervised manner, which was calculated through the R package GSVA. Briefly, the GSVA score represents the integrated level of the expression of a gene set, which is positively correlated with the gene set expression.
