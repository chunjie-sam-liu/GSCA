  • Methods

    • The expression and subtype analysis is used to find subtype relevant changes of gene expression. For this function, GSCA used clinical data of tumor samples from 9 cancer types (HNSC, LUSC, COAD, STAD, LUAD, GBM, BRCA, KIRC, BLCA). mRNA expression and clinical subtype data were merged by sample barcode. The subtype subgroup must have at least 5 samples. GSCA compared the GSVA score among groups through Wilcoxon test (number of subtype groups == 2) and ANOVA test (number of subtype groups > 2).

    Subtypes indicates molecular subtypes, if available, else indicates clustering subtypes.

  • Interpretation

    • GSCA provides two forms of results, including tables and figures.

    • Table:

      • The table presents detailed information about Cancer type, Gene symbol, P value and FDR.

    • GSCA provides result figure, including:
      • Bubble plot:

        • This plot presents the FDR through bubble color and size. The row is the gene set symbol and the column is the selected cancer types.
