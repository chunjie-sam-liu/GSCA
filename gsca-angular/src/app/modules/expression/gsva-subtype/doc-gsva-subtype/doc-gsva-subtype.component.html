  • Methods

    • The GSVA and subtype analysis is used to find subtype-relevant changes in gene set expression. For this function, GSCA used clinical data from tumor samples from 9 cancer types (HNSC, LUSC, COAD, STAD, LUAD, GBM, BRCA, KIRC, BLCA). GSVA score and clinical subtype data were merged by sample barcode. The subtype subgroup must have at least 5 samples. GSCA compared the GSVA score among groups through the Wilcoxon test (number of subtype groups == 2) and ANOVA test (number of subtype groups > 2).

    GSVA score represents the variation of gene set activity over a specific cancer sample population in an unsupervised manner, which was calculated through the R package GSVA. Briefly, the GSVA score represents the integrated level of the expression of a gene set, which is positively correlated with the gene set expression.

    Subtypes indicate molecular subtypes, if available, else indicates clustered subtypes.

  • Interpretation

    • GSCA provides two forms of results, including tables and figures.

    • Table:

      • The table presents detailed information about Cancer type, P value, and GSVA score in each subtype (Subtype1(mean/n)).

      P value: the statistical significance estimated by the Wilcoxon test (subtype groups == 2) and the ANOVA test (subtype groups > 2).

      Subtype(mean/n)) presents the subtype names, mean GSVA score, and number of samples.

    • Box plot:

      • Compares the GSVA score between subtypes of specific cancer.
